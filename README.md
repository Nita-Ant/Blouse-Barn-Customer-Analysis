# Blouse Barn Customer Analysis

## **Introduction**
Inspired by TV Series Schitt’s Creek, I wonder what kind of analysis could help Wendy Kurtz, owner of Blouse Barn—a clothing retailer shop—if her new hire, David Rose, were to use his Power BI skills to help Wendy make better business decisions (unlike what she did in the series).

I used a shopping behavior dataset and imagined it to be Blouse Barn’s data. I will be taking the role of David to provide key insights and recommendations to improve Blouse Barn’s profitability and repeat customer purchases.  

## **Key Insights**
-	The data shows that the core customer base of Blouse Barn are aged 20-70 years old with a demographic’s majority consisting of Men.
-	Customers aged 20–30 gave the highest ratings at 3.79 (1.07% above average). Conversely, despite being the highest spenders, the 50–60 age group gave the lowest ratings at 3.71 (1.07% below average).
-	Clothing generated the most profit followed by Accessories. However, there is a noticeable difference between their review ratings with Accessories being received better by customers.
-	Seasons are seen having prominent changes in review ratings and purchased items.
-	Strategies in highlighting visibility in marketing campaigns and product bundling with popular products might help in increasing the volume of sales for Accessories while using additional profit to research and improve Clothing’s quality to resolve its challenges.
-	Implementing targeted feedback incentives is recommended to understand preferences better. executing targeted marketing strategies designed for Women (i.e discounts during Women’s Month) is recommended to drive a stronger customer base in Women’s market.
-	Blouse Barn’s inventory management should be able to adjust to size preferences (with Medium as dominant choice) and different seasons (items that fit the season—Jackets during Fall and Sunglasses during Summer) to accommodate demands and seasonal changes.
